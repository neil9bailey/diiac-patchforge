# PatchForge Intended Use

## Product

DIIaC™ PatchForge

## Intended Use

DIIaC™ PatchForge is intended to support governed vulnerability, patch, protection, and remediation decision-making for IT and OT assets.

PatchForge helps authorised users compile source-bound evidence, assess service exposure, evaluate exploitability and patch feasibility, record human review, and produce signed decision artefacts for patch governance.

## Intended Users

PatchForge is intended for:

- security leads
- service owners
- CAB and emergency change participants
- enterprise IT governance teams
- MSP service managers
- operational resilience and assurance teams
- OT governance stakeholders
- customer assurance and audit teams

## Intended Environments

PatchForge is intended for organisations that need evidence-bound remediation governance across:

- customer-facing services
- internal enterprise IT services
- cloud-hosted systems
- managed service customer estates
- service-transition gates
- OT and critical infrastructure governance contexts

PatchForge may integrate with scanners, service catalogues, CMDBs, ITSM platforms, security tools, change records, and DIIaC™ IT Enterprise / IT Services. These integrations provide inputs and context; they do not change PatchForge into those source systems.

## Intended Decisions

PatchForge supports governed decision postures including:

- patch required
- emergency change required
- mitigate temporarily
- risk accept temporarily
- defer pending evidence
- block go-live
- patch not applicable
- close verified

## Intended Outputs

PatchForge is intended to produce:

- signed patch-decision packs
- CAB patch decision reports
- board vulnerability remediation summaries
- customer patch governance packs
- risk acceptance reports
- OT patch deferral reports
- evidence catalogues
- replay certificates and verification manifests

## Explicit Non-Intended Use

PatchForge is not intended to:

- scan environments for vulnerabilities
- generate exploit code or exploit instructions
- deploy patches
- mutate production systems
- replace ITSM, CMDB, SIEM/SOAR, EDR/XDR, or OT engineering systems
- autonomously approve CAB decisions
- autonomously accept risk
- certify that a system is secure, safe, or compliant by itself

## Human Accountability

PatchForge can compile evidence and decision context, but final approval requires accountable human review. Risk acceptance requires owner, rationale, expiry, and approval evidence.

SRA-assisted research is advisory only and remains pending review until an authorised human reviewer accepts, rejects, or supersedes the output.
