# PatchForge Product Boundary

## Boundary Statement

DIIaC™ PatchForge is a vulnerability, patch, protection, and remediation governance intelligence layer. It supports governed decision-making and evidence-bound reporting. It does not directly perform discovery, exploitation, remediation deployment, production mutation, or autonomous approval.

## What PatchForge Is

PatchForge is:

- a vulnerability and patch governance intelligence layer
- a patch decision-control centre
- a source-bound vulnerability evidence catalogue
- an SRA-assisted research and triage environment
- a CAB, service-owner, and security-lead decision workbench
- a signed patch-governance pack generator
- a proactive protection and exposure-control dashboard
- an add-on to DIIaC™ IT Enterprise / IT Services

## What PatchForge Is Not

PatchForge is not:

- a vulnerability scanner
- an exploit-generation system
- a patch deployment tool
- a SIEM or SOAR replacement
- an ITSM replacement
- a CMDB replacement
- an EDR or XDR replacement
- an OT engineering tool
- an autonomous CAB
- an autonomous risk-acceptance system

## Prohibited Capability Claims

PatchForge must not claim to:

- discover all vulnerabilities in an environment
- verify exploitability by generating exploit instructions
- deploy or orchestrate patches directly
- mutate production infrastructure
- replace customer ITSM, CMDB, SIEM/SOAR, EDR/XDR, or OT engineering systems
- satisfy CAB approval automatically
- issue final risk acceptance without accountable human approval
- certify that a system is safe, secure, or compliant by itself

## SRA Boundary

Any Security Research Agent capability must remain advisory only.

SRA output must be:

- source-bound
- traceable
- pending review by default
- unable to close hard evidence gates by itself
- unable to approve a risk acceptance
- unable to issue deployment instructions
- free from exploit steps and exploit-generation content

## Decision Boundary

PatchForge can recommend or compile governed decision postures such as:

- patch required
- emergency change required
- mitigate temporarily
- risk accept temporarily
- defer pending evidence
- block go-live
- patch not applicable
- close verified

Final approval remains a human governance action and must be recorded with accountable owner, role, date, evidence references, and decision rationale.

## Surface Requirements

This boundary must remain visible in:

- product documentation
- admin configuration
- UI footer or legal/help surfaces
- SRA Research page
- report templates
- signed decision packs
- customer-facing exports
